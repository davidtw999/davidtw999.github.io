Biography

I am a data scientist with mechatronics engineering background. I received a double degree in computer science and mechatronics engineering from the University of Melbourne, where I studied and built robot for robotcup competition in 2009. Dr Darwin Lau and I successfully designed and implemented a feedback system for path navigation on autonomous robot.

After graduation, I have worked as a IT support analyst, report analyst and data engineer from 2010 to 2017. During this period of work, my responsibility was to develop and write business reports. Use SQL and Excel to analyse business needs and problems, and creating electronic databases, analysing reports, helping to develop reporting software, finding ways to store company data effectively.

In 2017, I realised that due to the rapid development of software and hardware in the era of big data, more and more data-driven businesses are emerging. Therefore, I took a part-time study for the master's degree in Data Science at Monash University. In the meanwhile, I also worked as an operations manager at Umine, a blockchain startup. My responsibility was to lead the team to build mining software for cryptocurrency. We used public trading exchange transaction data and computing power to optimise mining efficiency.

Based on my previous work and study, I have used many statistical models and data visualisations to explore time series datasets for the financial and transportation industries. Currently, I am a Data Scientist worked for Broadspectrum at Monash Univerisity. I received my master's degree in Data Science in 2020 from Monash Univerisity. Nowadays, I am using Natural Langauage Process and Computer Vision technologies to develop more complex models in extracting and interpret patterns in multiple types of large-scale data.
